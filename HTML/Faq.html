Faq about Mardi Gras

No! Mardi Gras is a holiday, just like Christmas and Easter. It belongs to everyone. Do not believe the claims of businesses that offer "official" merchandise from Mardi Gras or the "official" Mardi Gras website. The use of the term "official" is just a way for vendors to sell their products or services to those who do not know better. Products or websites are not licensed by the government for Mardi Gras. Each single parade is responsible for all its merchandise. In some parts of the state, official organizations control their Mardi Gras celebration, but this is not true in New Orleans.

The weather during Mardi Gras can vary from very cold to very hot, almost hot. Sometimes this will change from one end to the other during the same day. It is advisable to lay down the clothes as a cold morning can turn into a hot afternoon. Because you will spend long days outside, pack comfortable shoes and clothes that may get a little dirty. Do not forget to check the forecast for rain. If you forecast storms then you will definitely want to bring a raincoat!.

In every way, yes! Contrary to popular belief, Mardi Gras is a family celebration. Those of us who grew up in New Orleans feel guilty once our kids have grown up and we keep going to every parade because we used to use the “take the kids” patch! Bring large bags (even large garbage bags!) To hold all the things they will grab. Injections often include toys, stuffed animals, beads and more. It is recommended to take your children to parades in suburban areas like Matayar, which is 10 minutes away.

The best transportation methods will be through taxi / car sharing services, tram or rental car, depending on where you are staying. Driving your vehicle and finding parking spaces will be difficult.

Mardi Gras' date changes each year as it is connected to Easter, which can take place every Sunday from March 23 to April 25. Mardi Gras Day always takes place 47 days before Easter.
