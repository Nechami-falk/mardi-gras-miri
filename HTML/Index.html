Who is Mardi Gras?

It is recommended to have some kind of costume in the spirit of the festivities, and it is recommended that it be in the following colors: green, purple and gold. Not during the carnival, the city is full of tourists, so you should book accommodation well in advance. The hotels have been accepting reservations since August, for a minimum of four nights. On the morning of the carnival, it is recommended to arrive early to get a good vantage point for the parades. Don't forget to bring a camera!

Who is it for?

For lovers of street celebrations and debauchery, for all those for whom food, drink and dancing do it, and for all jazz lovers who dream of visiting a real jazz kingdom combined with a mind-blowing party.

How do you prepare?

The French Quarter of the city of New Orleans, the largest of the cities of the state of Louisiana in the south of the United States, becomes for two weeks a year a focus of unbridled parties and celebrations. The famous carnival, the Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday in French), which is held in many places in the world, reaches in New Orleans a height of beauty and joy unmatched anywhere else. The wonderful carnival of New Orleans, which was first started in 1857 by French expatriates, is a global attraction for thousands of locals and tourists who plan their arrival in the city many months in advance.
